# Life loops

Keep the person as subject, not optimization object.

Ask:

- Whose goal is this, and may the goal be changed or abandoned?
- Is a loop better than rest, acceptance, a conversation, or professional help?
- Who benefits, who pays, and who did not consent?
- Which proxy could improve while real life gets worse?
- What evidence would change the recommendation?
- What is the smallest reversible experiment?
- What is the graduation condition that ends the loop?

Decision loops separate facts, estimates, preferences, and unknowns and include no-action. Habit loops change cue, environment, and action size before blaming willpower. Life reviews start from observations, test stories against counterexamples, and choose at most one bounded experiment.

Never diagnose, prescribe, moralize a streak, issue a life verdict from a weighted score, or create permanent dependence on the loop.
