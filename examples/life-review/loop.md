# Monthly life review

Decision owner: user. Memory: off unless explicitly enabled.

1. Gather calendar events, notes, and user-provided observations for the month.
2. Label each statement as observation, interpretation, or uncertainty.
3. Ask: what energized me, what repeatedly cost more than expected, who was affected, and which commitment no longer serves its purpose?
4. Find counterexamples to the strongest story about the month.
5. Select one bounded experiment, one thing to continue, and one thing to stop or defer.
6. Set a review date and end the loop.

The output is partial if evidence is sparse. It must not diagnose the user or convert every life area into a metric.
