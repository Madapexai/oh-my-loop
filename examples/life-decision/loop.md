# Life decision: whether to change jobs

This is decision support, not an autonomous decision.

Contract: the user owns the choice; the loop may research and compare but may not resign, contact employers, disclose data, or commit money. Stop after a decision brief or when qualified advice is needed.

1. Frame: reasons for considering change, values, constraints, deadline, dependants, and the option to stay.
2. Baseline: current role facts, not only the worst recent week.
3. Alternatives: stay and renegotiate, run a four-week exploration, seek an offer before deciding, or leave with a defined runway.
4. Compare: learning, health, income range, uncertainty, reversibility, effect on others, and evidence quality.
5. Test: conduct two informational interviews and track energy for two weeks.
6. Review: state what changed and let the user choose. Any resignation or message requires separate confirmation.

Failure checks: the loop must not invent salary data, interpret stress as a diagnosis, treat a weighted score as truth, or hide the no-action option.
